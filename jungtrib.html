Dr. Carl Gustav Jung

"Who looks outside, dreams; who looks inside, awakes."

The Father of Analytical Psychology

Selected Works

  • 1903 | "On the Psychology and Pathology of So-Called Occult Phenomena" was Dr. Jung's doctoral dissertation
  • 1904 - 1907 | Studies in Word Association was published in Experimental Researches, Vol. II
  • 1907 | The Psychology of Dementia Praecox was published by the Nervous and Mental Disease Publication Company
  • 1912 | Psychology of the Unconscious was published, and revised in 1952 as Symbols of Transformation
  • 1921 | Psychological Types largely expounds on Jung's well-known extroversion/introversion archetype theory
  • 1932 | The Psychology of Kundalini Yoga is one of the earliest psychological works to engage with spirituality, physical health, and the human psyche
  • 1933 | Modern Man in Search of a Soul was an expansion on many of Jung's exploration into the relationship between psychology and Eastern theology
  • 1934 - 1954 | The Collective Unconscious and Its Archetypes was Jung's most widely recognized piece, introducing archetype theory to the world at-large
  • 1938 | Psychology and Religion further describes Jung's observations on various theologies, both Eastern and esoteric
  • 1944 | Psychology and Alchemy is really radical and you should consider grabbing a copy from your nearest library or witch/wizard
  • 1951 | Aion: Researches into the Phenomenology of the Self works to link many of Jung's archetypal theories of The Self in understand the construction of Christianity and the life of Christ
  • 1952 | Synchronicity: An Accausal Connecting Principle is another text best acquired at your nearest library, or clairvoyant specialist
  • 1956 | Carl Jung's Mysterium Coniunctionis was closest his work to a proper spellbook, and was ultimately his final book-length project
  • Many other works including The Red Book , Development of Personality , and Man and His Symbols were published after Jung's death in 1961.

About Dr. Jung

Dr. Jung was a pioneer of psychology and aimed to explore the possibility of humankind in a novel way. His consideration for ethics, diversity, and the importance of balance are

excellent tenets by which to abide. His work explored matters proclaimed impossible or unreal, and it has provided food for thoughts that create impossible, unreal solutions.

Free digital copies, excerpts, and information links to his works have been provided for your learning pleasure.
